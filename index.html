Demos from "Voice Reconstruction from Silent Speech with a Sequence-to-Sequence Model "

Abstract: Silent Speech Decoding (SSD) based on surface electromyography (sEMG) has become a prevalent task of brain-computer interface in recent years. Although progresses have been made to decode sEMG to audio successfully, some problems still remain. In this paper, we propose an optimized sequence-to-sequence (Seq2Seq) approach to synthesize voice from sEMG based silent speech. Both sEMG based silent and vocal speech are collected and preprocessed to provide training information. Then, we extract duration information using the alignment between the silent and the vocal speech signals to regulate the sEMG based silent speech following audio length. Furthermore, we use a phoneme classification and sEMG based vocal reconstruction modules to improve the model performance. Finally, experiments on the dataset generated by a Mandarin Chinese speaker, which consists of 6.49 hours of measurements, demonstrate that the proposed model improves the mapping accuracy and the sound quality of reconstructed voice.
